FAQ's

To store seeds properly, keep them in a cool, dry, and dark place. Use airtight containers to protect seeds from moisture and pests. Label the containers with the seed type and date of collection for easy identification.

The viability of seeds depends on the type of seed and storage conditions. Some seeds can remain viable for several years if stored properly. Generally, most seeds will last 1-5 years, but it is best to check specific recommendations for each type.

To test seed viability, place a few seeds between damp paper towels and keep them in a warm place. Check for germination after a few days to a week. If most seeds sprout, they are still viable; if only a few or none sprout, they may no longer be good for planting.

The best time to plant seeds depends on the type of plant and your local climate. Generally, seeds are planted in early spring or late summer. Refer to the seed packet for specific planting times and conditions.

Plant seeds at a depth of two to three times their diameter. Some small seeds should be sown on the surface and lightly pressed into the soil, while larger seeds may need to be planted deeper. Always refer to the seed packet for specific planting instructions.
