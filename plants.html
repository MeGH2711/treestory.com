FAQ's

The frequency of watering indoor plants depends on the type of plant, the pot size, and environmental conditions such as light and humidity. A general rule of thumb is to water when the top inch of soil is dry. Some plants prefer consistently moist soil, while others like succulents need less frequent watering.

Most indoor plants thrive in bright, indirect light. However, the specific light requirements vary by plant species. For instance, succulents and cacti prefer direct sunlight, while ferns and snake plants can tolerate lower light conditions.

To prevent pests, keep your plants healthy by providing proper care, such as adequate watering, light, and nutrients. Inspect new plants for pests before bringing them indoors, and regularly check your existing plants for signs of infestation. Isolate affected plants and treat them with natural or chemical pest control methods as needed.

Yellow leaves can be caused by various factors, including overwatering, underwatering, poor drainage, lack of light, nutrient deficiencies, or pest infestations. Evaluate the plant's care conditions and make necessary adjustments to address the issue.

Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer specifically formulated for indoor plants. Follow the instructions on the fertilizer package for the correct dilution and frequency. Typically, fertilizing once a month during the growing season (spring and summer) is sufficient, while reducing or stopping fertilization during the dormant season (fall and winter).
